About DISHA Foundation

Social inequality is the manifestation of human disintegration that results in various issues like communal allures, caste-based discrimination, patriarchy, poverty, and unemployment. DISHA Foundation was registered on 17th July 1996 under the Society Registration Act 1958 to combat these problems.

Vision

We envisage an environment where every individual must feel capable, secure, and have dignity with respect.

Mission

The foundation is dedicated to enabling people to confront vulnerability and inequality through partnership and strategic alliances.

Objectives

  • Improve the status of women, children, and adolescents through various schemes and projects.
  • Develop a socio-economic perspective for vulnerable communities.
  • Encourage communities to conserve natural resources and address environmental issues.
  • Promote livelihood and women's entrepreneurship through alternative technologies.
  • Advocate for policy change via networking and alliances.
  • Promote initiatives for quality education.

Training and Capacity Building

We conduct various trainings to empower individuals to support development initiatives:

  • PRI training on gender behavior and budgeting.
  • Training rickshaw pullers in bird sanctuary behavior.
  • Vision building for youth in colleges and training institutes.
  • Life skills training under the Population Development Program.
  • RCH training, child rights, and gender sensitization programs.
  • Trainings for SHGs, youth on masculinity, and child labor volunteers.
